The Day That Drove Nowhere

A Patricia Moment
Patricia is planning her recruiting day.

Patricia woke up this morning with a strong feeling about which daycares to visit. She did not call ahead. She did not check her notes from last week. She drove 45 minutes to a location that was closed for a private event. Patricia is now in the parking lot eating a granola bar and reconsidering her choices.

Scene 1 of 6
Monday, 8:45 AM

The week starts with a plan. Sort of.

A recruiter gets in her car with a mental list of locations she wants to hit this week. No specific order. No calls made ahead of time. The plan is to drive around and see what's happening — which is a plan, technically, in the same way that "I'll figure it out when I get there" is a plan.

She's been doing this for a few weeks. Some days are great. Most days are whatever the daycare gods have decided.

Monday, 9:15 AM

First stop. Four dogs. None eligible.

She drives 25 minutes to her first location. Four dogs in daycare — all too young, recently donated, or medically ineligible. The staff is friendly. It's a complete zero for recruiting purposes.

She didn't call ahead. If she had, she'd have known this was a boarding-heavy week and planned accordingly. Instead she spent 50 minutes round-trip to rule out a location she could have ruled out in 45 seconds on the phone.

Monday, 11:00 AM

Second stop. Closed for a staff training day.

She pulls into the parking lot. Sign on the door: "Closed today for team training. See you tomorrow!"

This information was available. It was on their Instagram. It was something a 20-second call Monday morning would have surfaced. Instead it's a 30-minute round trip and a parking lot.

Monday, 12:30 PM

Third stop. Five eligible dogs. Twenty minutes to work with.

She finally hits a location with real potential — five eligible dogs, staff who know her, two owners present who've been meaning to sign up. But she's already running behind, has an appointment at 1:30, and only has time to leave materials and promise to follow up.

60 miles driven · 1 solid lead · 3 hours gone · best stop rushed
The Same Week, Friday

Same recruiter. Different approach.

She spends Thursday afternoon making five calls. Asks each location: how many dogs do you have in daycare right now? Any eligible dogs you've seen recently? Are you heavy on boarders this week?

Friday morning she has a route — three stops in the same corridor, all confirmed to have activity worth visiting. She has specific dogs to ask about at two of them by name.

Monday

60 miles, reactive, best opportunity rushed, 1 lead

Friday

Planned route, confirmed stops, specific leads, home by 2pm with 4 sign-ups

Same recruiter. Same territory. The only difference was five phone calls the day before.
