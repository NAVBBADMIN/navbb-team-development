A Patricia Moment
Patricia is taking this self-assessment.

Patricia gave herself a 5 on location knowledge because she has been to all of her locations at least once. Patricia also cannot name the top-producing location in her territory without looking it up. Patricia sees no contradiction here. Patricia considers her schedule to be her strategy. Her schedule is alphabetical.

Question 1 of 6 · Location Knowledge
I can name my top three locations by sign-up rate without looking anything up.
Not your favorite, not the most convenient — the three that actually produce the most new donors.
Question 2 of 6 · Location Knowledge
I know the logistics of each location I visit regularly — indoor vs. outdoor pickup, best days to go, who to talk to.
Not what's on the schedule — what you'd tell a new recruiter about each location before they walked in the door.
Question 3 of 6 · Prioritization
When I plan my week, I deliberately allocate more time to locations that need it — not just locations that are convenient or comfortable.
Developing locations should get more intentional visits than anchors that are already performing.
Question 4 of 6 · Prioritization
I have at least one location I've honestly evaluated and decided isn't worth the same level of investment as others.
This is a hard one. It means acknowledging that time spent at a low-return location is time not spent somewhere better.
Question 5 of 6 · Strategic Thinking
If a visit cancels unexpectedly, I know immediately where I'd redirect that time.
Not "figure it out" — an actual location in mind that you've been thinking about developing or pushing.
Question 6 of 6 · Strategic Thinking
I can describe what it would take to move one of my developing locations to anchor status — specifically, not generally.
"More visits" isn't specific enough. What's the actual thing missing?
