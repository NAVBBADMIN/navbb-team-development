Claim One Thing

Do this in real life

This is the only piece this week that happens outside the screen. Everything else was reading and reflecting — this one wants an actual result from your actual job.

You've spent this week thinking about Patricia and her printer shrine. Now it's time to make sure you're not quietly doing the same thing somewhere. Somewhere in your week, right now, there's a small thing sitting in a gray area — something that's nobody's clearly defined job, that you've half-noticed, that you've been assuming someone else has. This task is simple: find it, and take it.

The Mission

Find one gray-area thing. Claim it. Close it out.

  1. Identify one small task or loose end this week that doesn't clearly belong to anyone — something you've noticed but assumed wasn't yours to fix.
  2. Say out loud (or in writing, to whoever's relevant) that you've got it. Not "I'll look into it" — "I've got this."
  3. Actually do it, or get it meaningfully moving, before the week's sync.
  4. Fill out the short report below so you've got something real to share.
Guardrails
  • Keep it small. This isn't about taking on a giant new project — it's about practicing the reflex on something low-stakes.
  • If it touches someone else's clear responsibility, loop them in rather than quietly taking over — ownership isn't the same as overstepping.
  • If you genuinely can't find a gray-area task this week, that's worth noting too — bring that observation instead.
