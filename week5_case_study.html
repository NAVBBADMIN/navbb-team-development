Scene 1 of 6
Tuesday Afternoon — The Lobby

Same daycare. Same dogs. Same owners. Two recruiters.

It's a Tuesday afternoon at a mid-sized daycare. The lobby is active — dogs coming out, owners checking phones, staff moving between the front desk and the back. Two recruiters are both working this location on the same day, trading off on different weeks. This week it's Patricia. Next week it'll be Recruiter B.

Both have been here before. The difference is what each one carries in when they walk through the door.

Patricia — 3:45pm

Patricia has her flyer ready before she's through the door.

Patricia scans the lobby, identifies three dog owners waiting, and approaches the first one. She introduces herself, explains the program, describes the benefits, and hands over a flyer. The owner nods politely. Patricia moves to the second person.

Patricia's approach

"Hi there — I'm with NAVBB, the canine blood bank. Is that your dog over there? We're actually looking for donors — healthy dogs 55 pounds and up, between one and seven years old. Your dog would be helping save other dogs' lives, and it's completely safe. Here's some information."

The owner takes the flyer. Their dog comes out of the back. The flyer goes into their bag. Patricia is already talking to person number two — the same speech, word for word.

0 of 3 owners Patricia approached who remembered being spoken to by a recruiter last month. Not because it didn't happen — because the conversation wasn't memorable enough to stick.
Recruiter B — The Following Tuesday

She checks her notes before she gets out of the car.

Recruiter B pulls up her notes from the last visit before she walks in. Three people she remembers: Dave, whose golden Rocky is a great candidate but whose wife wasn't sure. A woman she spoke to briefly whose dog just turned one — might be eligible now. A man who asked a lot of questions but said he needed to talk to his vet first.

She walks in knowing exactly what she's walking into. Not a lobby of strangers — a lobby of people she's already started a conversation with.

The First Two Minutes

She spots Dave. She doesn't launch into the program.

Dave is at the front desk signing something. She waits until he turns around, then makes eye contact and says:

Recruiter B's opening

"Hey Dave — did you get a chance to talk to your wife about Rocky? I know last time you said she might have questions."

Dave's face shifts immediately. He wasn't expecting her to remember. He tells her his wife actually looked it up online and has a couple of questions — and pulls out his phone right there to text her.

She didn't pitch. She picked up the thread. That's the whole difference.

End of the Visit

One sign-up. One solid warm lead. One new connection.

The woman whose dog just turned one — her name is Keisha — signs Rocky up after a ten-minute conversation that started with: "Is that the puppy you mentioned last time? She looks like she grew about six inches."

The man who wanted to talk to his vet had done it. He's not ready to commit yet but wants to come to the next donation event to see what it looks like. She gives him the date.

Dave's wife texts back with two questions. Recruiter B answers them on the spot, and Dave says he'll register Rocky that night.

Patricia — Last Tuesday

Three conversations. Three flyers distributed. Zero sign-ups. Zero follow-up threads to pick up next time. Zero people who remembered being spoken to.

Recruiter B — This Tuesday

Three conversations. No flyers distributed unprompted. One sign-up. One pending registration. One donor event attendee. Three people who will recognize her name next time she walks in.

Your Turn

Think of someone you've spoken to more than once who still hasn't signed up. What do you remember about where you left off?

Not what their dog looks like — what did they say? What was the hesitation? What were they going to do before they could say yes? If you can't remember, that's the answer to why they're still a maybe. What would you say if you saw them this week?
