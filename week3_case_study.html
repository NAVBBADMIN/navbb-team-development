Same Location, Different Recruiter

Scene 1 of 6
Tuesday Morning

Two recruiters. One location. Same day.

It's a Tuesday in July. Two NAVBB recruiters are both scheduled to visit the same daycare — Paws & Play, a mid-sized facility they've each been to before. Same location, same day, same pool of dogs. What happens next is completely different.

This isn't about who worked harder. It's about what each one was paying attention to.

Recruiter A — Patricia

Patricia arrives with no information and leaves with the same amount.

Patricia pulls into the parking lot at 10am. She has been here four times. She has no notes from the previous visits because she doesn't take notes. She walks in, counts eight dogs, attempts to recruit the eligible ones, gets two soft maybes, and leaves by 10:40.

On the way out she passes the owner's golden retriever — a beautiful, healthy 4-year-old she has seen every single visit — and does not mention it. She didn't know the dog's name. She didn't know the owner's name. She assumed someone else probably handled it already.

0 of 4 visits where Patricia asked a question that wasn't directly about recruiting a specific dog in front of her
Recruiter B — Same Location, 11am

She walks in with three questions already in her head.

Before leaving the house, Recruiter B checked her notes from the last visit. She remembered that Tuesday mornings run light on daycare dogs but heavy on regulars — and regulars are the better donation candidates. She also remembered that a staff member mentioned a "regular" who brings two labs every week.

She walks in knowing what she expects to find. Her three questions for the visit: Are the labs here today? What days do you get the most daycare traffic? And who's the owner of that golden retriever I keep seeing?

Recruiter B — The Conversation

One extra question changes the whole visit.

The labs aren't there. But when she asks about them, the staff member lights up — turns out the owner is incredibly proud of those dogs and is already interested in donation. She's just never been asked directly. The staff member offers to introduce them next time they're in.

The golden retriever's owner is in the parking lot. She asks. The owner hadn't heard of NAVBB. Ten minutes later she has a completed intake form and a scheduled appointment.

Neither of those things were in the building when she walked in. They came from asking one question that wasn't on a script.

End of Tuesday

Same location. Same Tuesday. Completely different results.

Patricia

2 soft maybes. No new contacts. No new information about the location. Will return next week and repeat the same visit for the fifth time.

Recruiter B

1 completed intake. 1 warm introduction for next visit. A mental model of the location she'll carry into every future visit. And a name she can use when she walks in the door.

The difference wasn't time. It wasn't talent. It was one recruiter who paid attention across multiple visits and one who treated every visit like the first.

Your Turn

Think of a location you've visited more than twice. What do you actually know about it?

Not what you saw on the last visit — what's the pattern? What do you know about the staff, the regulars, the owner, the busiest days? If you can't answer those, what's the one question you'd ask on your next visit to start building that picture?
