Question 1 of 6 · Preparation
Before a field visit, I review who I spoke to last time and what we talked about.
Not just who was there — what was their hesitation, where did the conversation end, what were they going to do next.
Question 2 of 6 · Preparation
When I hand someone a flyer, it's because they specifically need it — not because I give one to everyone.
Flyers go to people who need something to take home for a spouse or vet conversation. Not to everyone who makes eye contact.
Question 3 of 6 · Reading the Room
I can tell within the first few seconds whether someone is open to being approached right now.
Not whether they'll say yes to the program — whether this is the right moment to start the conversation at all.
Question 4 of 6 · Reading the Room
When someone changes their energy — gets distracted, goes shorter in their answers, glances away — I adjust what I'm doing.
Not finishing the pitch and hoping they come back. Actually changing course.
Question 5 of 6 · Follow-Through
When I return to a location, I specifically pick up threads from the last conversation — not just who's there today.
In-person follow-up: "Did you get a chance to talk to your vet?" Not a call or text. The next time you see them.
Question 6 of 6 · Follow-Through
I can name at least one "maybe" from the past month who I have a plan to follow up with in person on my next visit.
Not someone you hope shows up — someone you know their hesitation, their situation, and what you're going to say.
